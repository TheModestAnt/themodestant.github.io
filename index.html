📚 SACRED TERMINOLOGY

THE BREAD
GLOSSARY

Words you must know. Terms you must understand. Language shared between those who take this seriously, and those who should.

CRUMB The interior structure of a loaf. Judged by its openness, its consistency, and the quiet confidence it projects. An even, open crumb is the goal of a well-lived life.
CRUST The outer shell. Sacred. Non-negotiable. The crust is not the problem. You are the problem. Learn to love it.
STARTER A live culture of wild yeast and bacteria. It requires regular feeding. It has moods. It has preferences. Name it. Respect it. Do not neglect it.
PROVING The resting phase in which dough rises. This takes time. That is the point. Impatience at this stage produces a dense, joyless loaf that reflects its maker.
SCORING The cuts made across the surface before baking. They control how the loaf expands. Score with a single, decisive motion. Hesitation is visible in the final product.
THE HEEL The end piece of a loaf. Frequently discarded. Deeply misunderstood. The heel is the crust at its most concentrated. Eating it is an act of respect.
HYDRATION The ratio of water to flour. Higher hydration yields a more open crumb and a more difficult dough. It is also a philosophy. How much difficulty are you willing to handle for a better result?
MAILLARD The chemical reaction between amino acids and sugars that produces the golden crust and complex flavour during baking. It is science. It is also divine. These are not contradictory.
